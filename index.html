INTRODUCTION

Unemployment had always been a major issue in Assam. It’s 2020, but the issue remains unsolved. There are various factors that contribute to the high rate of unemployment in the state. Many attempts have been made by the state and central government to rectify this issue but those attempts turned out to be unfruitful. The number of job seekers is considerably high and is expected to grow further. Unemployment also leads to various other issues. So, solving this issue may also solve other related issues. And in order to achieve that goal, proper planning and implementation of newer and efficient methods and dedication is required.

Unemployment is a complex issue with various causes. However it is possible to identify the main causes of unemployment and find out measures to address these issues. In this article, we will discus every minor to major factors that causes unemployment and some measures to rectify them. We will also look at some survey data published by the government and different agencies. The main goal is to create a society where each and every person can find a sustainable livelihood for themselves and their families. So, let’s get started.

What is Unemployment?

A person is said to be unemployed if he/she, despite being physically and mentally fit to work, is unable to find a job. The economy of a country is highly dependent upon employment. High rate of unemployment leads to economic distress. According to the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), “Unemployment is persons above a specified age (usually above 15) not being in paid employment or self-employment but currently available for work during the reference period.”

Types of Unemployment

Before discussing about unemployment issue and it’s causes, let’s discuss about the types of unemployment. The following are some of the major types of Unemployment:

Rural Unemployment

Around 85.90 percent of Assam’s population live in Rural areas. But, there are not enough employment opportunities in those rural areas. This, gives rise to unemployment rate.

Seasonal unemployment

As stated above, Around 85.90 percent of Assam’s population live in Rural areas. So, a large population of Assam is highly dependent upon agricultural. But, they can’t grow their crops all year round. During off-season, they go completely unemployed.

Urban Unemployment

With more and more people moving to urban area in search of jobs, urban unemployment is also growing.

Industrial Unemployment

People who are willing to work in factories or industries in urban areas but cannot find work fall in this category.

Educated Unemployment

People who are educated but are unable to find a job that best matches their qualifications fall in this category. With the growing literacy rates, educated unemployment is also growing.

Technological Unemployment

This type of unemployment occurs every time a new technology arrives and the existing workforce are unable to cope with the new technology. They become unemployed when they are unable to adapt to the new technology. This type is unemployment is constantly growing with technological advancements.

Effects of unemployment

The major effects of unemployment are given below:

Impact on Individuals

  1. Mental health: Those who are unable to get a job despite having the required qualifications, undergo some mental health problems.
  2. Living standards: The unemployed people can not afford or access some essential services which lowers their standards of living.
  3. Life expectancy: The unemployed people are unable to afford healthcare facilities which shortens their lifespan.

Impact on Society

  1. Literacy: The absence of income created by unemployment can force families to deny educational opportunities to their children and deprive the economy of those future skills.
  2. Crime rates: The unemployed people can not afford or access some essential services which lowers their standards of living.
  3. Life expectancy: Studies have revealed that a unemployed person may involve in criminal activities to meet their economic needs.

Moreover, Unemployment also effects the overall economy of the state. It has a significant and negative effect on per capita GDP (which is basically the average income earned by a person in a given area in a specific year) in long-run and short-run.

Why is it important to solve unemployment issue?

  1. To raise the living standards of people.
  2. To boost the economy of the state.
  3. To increase affordability of healthcare facilities.
  4. To reduce robbery, burglary and other criminal activities.
  5. To increase the literacy rate of the state.
  6. To reduce mental heath issues caused by unemployment.

Assam: A brief introduction

Tea garden in Assam

The state of Assam is located in the north-eastern region of India. Assam is known for it’s Assam tea and Assam silk. Assam was the first site for oil drilling in Asia. India’s second largest hydrocarbon exploration and production public sector company has its operational headquarters in Duliajan of Assam. It is also known as the “Gateway to North East” because it connects all the north-eastern states with rest of India via Siliguri Corridor. It is also emerging as “The gateway to South-east Asia.” Kaziranga National Park and Manas National Park are two World Heritage Sites located in Assam.

Demographics

According to 2011 census, the total population of Assam was 31,169,272. The total population of the state has increased from 26,638,407 to 31,169,272 in the last ten years with a growth rate of 16.93%. The population of Assam is expected to reach 34.18  million by 2021 and 35.60 million by 2026.

Economy

The economy of Assam is based on Agriculture and Oil. Assam produces produces more than half of total tea production of India. The Assam-Arakan basin holds about a quarter of the country's oil reserves, and produces about 12% of its total petroleum. Per capita GDP of Assam is estimated to be ₹6,157 at constant prices (1993–94) and ₹10,198 at current prices, which is almost 40% lower than that in India. According to the recent estimates, per capita income in Assam has reached ₹6756 (1993–94 constant prices) in 2004–05, which is still much lower than India's.

The employment market in Assam

The population censuses, different quinquennial rounds of National sample Survey on employment data, Employment and Unemployment Surveys conducted by the Labour Bureau and Economic Census provides data on employment structure and workforce as well as involvement of human resources in economic activity, employment etc. On the other hand, the data available from the Employment Exchanges does not give a clear picture on unemployment as a large number of unemployed people either did not register their names with Employment Exchanges or those who secure employment through other channels do not delete their names from the records of Employment Exchanges.

The Census of India collects information on economic activity of an individual and classifies workers into two groups namely, Main workers and Marginal workers. Main workers are those who had worked for the major part of the reference period i.e. six months or more and Marginal workers are those who had worked less than six months. Unemployment in Assam: Overview

Unemployment has always been a major problem in Assam. Although, the GDP and the Net State Domestic Product (NSDP) of the state expanded between 2011-12 and 2016-17, the job market in Assam is quiet disappointing. According to a report published in October 2017 by the North Eastern Development Finance Corporation Limited (NEDFL), over 1.75 lakh jobs will be required by the state annually till 2020. However, the report didn't include employment in agriculture and plantation farm sectors and government offices. So, this requirement is even more. Employment in Assam is not growing at the same pace of mushrooming young population. Therefore, Assam is currently facing a twin issue.

Current scenario

According to report published by the State Skill, Employment and Entrepreneurship Department on November 2018, there are a total of 19,63,376 registered unemployed in the state, out of which 16,65,866 are educated or skilled and 2,97,510 are unskilled.

The report also stated that there are 942 unemployed medical graduates, 7,804 jobless engineering graduates, 327 jobless veterinary graduates, 832 jobless agri graduates, 16,576 jobless postgraduates, 3,17,8 24 general graduates, 6,82,796 HS-passed candidates, 5,67,340 HSLC-passed candidates, 9,167 diploma holders, 16,121 ITI certificate holders and 46,137 others in the state.

Causes of Unemployment in Assam

The following are the main causes of unemployment in Assam:

lack of Skills

The fact that a large number of people are unemployed is mainly because they do not have the skills required for a job.

Immigration

People can move from any state to any state in search of job. Infact, many people from Assam have moved to different states of India to work. So, should immigration be termed as a cause of unemployment in Assam? But, the real question is whether there are enough jobs in the state to employ its own people? Answer is a Big No. There are not adequate jobs to employ people of the state. But, every year many people move to Assam from other neighbouring states of India as well as neighbouring countries.

Population growth

The population of Assam grew from 3.29 million in 1901 to 6.70 million in 1941. It increased to 14.63 million in 1971 and 22.41 million in 1991. It increased to 26,655,528 in 2001 and 31,205,576 in 2011. Which means it grew by a rate of 17.1% in 2011. But, the employment rate did not grow at the same rate. Hence, unemployment rate kept on rising.

lack of adequate fund-allocation

Improper governmental programs targeting the unemployed population can harm the employment situation of the state. Lack of commitment to adress the job crisis causes employment to fall.

Role of Government

The government can play a major role in solving the job crisis in the states. Finding and implementing methods to solve this issue is completely in the hands of the government. But, due to lack of commitment, unemployment remained unsolved.

Slow rate of economic growth

Slow economic growth also causes unemployment. Slow economic growth implies that there are not ample jobs to absorb the unemployed people who are able and willing to work.

Suggestions to Solve Unemployment Problem

Although, unemployment is a complex issue, it's not impossible to to rectify it. Proper planning and implementation of better methods in a productive way may definitely solve this issue. Given below are some methods that could be helpful:

Change in education system

It’s very important to change the current educational pattern. Students who are interested in higher studies should be admitted in colleges and universities. Qualified engineers should be encouraged to start their own small units.

Probable outcome: An improved and better education patter will increase the competence among the youth of Assam and enable them to provide employment to others.

Policy regarding seasonal unemployment

Seasonal unemployment occurs in agriculture sector and agro based industries. In order to solve this, this following should be done:

  1. Agriculture should have multiple cropping,
  2. Plantations, horticulture, dairying and animal husbandry should be encouraged,
  3. Cottage industries should be encouraged.

Probable outcome: This will reduce seasonal unemployment as farmers will be able to involve in agricultural activities all year round.

Change in industrial technique

Production technique should match with the needs and means of the state. Capital intensive technology should be replaced with Labour intensive technology.

Probable outcome: Labour intensive technology require more people so this can help increase employment opportunities.

Expansion of Employment exchanges

More employment exchanges should be set up. Information regarding employment opportunities should be given to people.

Probable outcome: This will facilitate the unemployed people to find a suitable job and industries to find suitable manpower.

Setting up skill development center

North-east skill development center is located at Garchuk, Guwahati. But, that is not enough to train every unskilled worker of Assam. More such centers should be opened in different parts of the state.

Probable outcome: Those who are unskilled, will be able to reach these centers and get trained which will help them get a job.

More assistance to self employed people

People who are engaged in agriculture, trade, cottage and small scale industries etc. should be helped financially, providing raw materials and technical training.

Probable outcome: Proving assistance and resources will not only empower those who are self-employed but will also encourage others to choose self-employment.

Full and more productive employment

The main objective of states’s employment policy should be to increase employment opportunities and productivity of labour. Govt. should adopt a policy that provides employment to all people.

Probable outcome: Adopting better policies will bring employment opportunities for a large number of people.

Increase in Production

It is important to increase production in agriculture and industrial sectors in order to increase employment. Development of small and cottage industries should be encouraged.

Probable outcome: When production increases, the needs for workers also increases. So, increasing production will give rise to unemployment opportunities.

High rate of capital formation

Rate of capital formation in the country should be accelerated. Capital formation should be particularly encouraged in such activities which generate greater employment opportunities. Capital output ratio should be kept low.

Probable outcome: When production increases, the needs for workers also increases. So, increasing production will give rise to unemployment opportunities.

Industries in co-operative sector

The Govt. should take necessary steps to encourage Industries in co-operative sector in the state. This could help solve unemployment issue up to a great extent.

Probable outcome: Kerala Govt. set up a textile mill covering 600 unemployed persons on co-operative basis. Following this step may give similar results in Assam.

Encouraging start-ups

The government should encourage the youth to have their own start-up ideas. They should be provided with proper training and funds to implement their ideas.

Probable outcome: Those who have any start-up idea, will be able to provide employment to those who are willing to work.

Decentralisation of industrial activity

The Govt. should adopt policies which encourage decentralisation of industrial activity. Decentralisation of Industrial activity is necessary to reduce unemployment. There will be less employment opportunities in the under developed areas if industrial activities are centralised at one place.

Probable outcome: Decentralisation will increase industrial activities in the state which will increase the need of workforce.

Population control

In order to solve unemployment issue, the growth of population should be kept in control. Family planning programme should be implemented in an effective manner.

Probable outcome: Keeping population in check will help the government lower unemployment rate of the state.

Designate tax free zones

The should open up Special economic zone that will offer the foreign investors tax and customs incentives. This method is used by other Indian states like Goa, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh, Odisha, etc.

Probable outcome: Offering tax incentives will attract foreign investors. More investments in the state will generate jobs for the unemployed people.
